Multiple vulnerabilities have been reported in various industrial control systems and products, including Mitsubishi Electric, Crestron, HID Global, SystemK, Delta Electronics, Rockwell Automation, Chirp Systems, and AVEVA Edge. These vulnerabilities could allow attackers to access sensitive data, execute arbitrary code, escalate privileges, and bypass authentication. CISA has assigned CVSS v3.1 base scores to these vulnerabilities and recommends organizations to implement cybersecurity strategies, report any suspicious activity, and take defensive measures to minimize the risk of exploitation. No known public exploitation targeting these vulnerabilities has been reported at this time. Additional mitigation guidance and recommended practices are available on the ICS webpage for some of the vulnerabilities.
