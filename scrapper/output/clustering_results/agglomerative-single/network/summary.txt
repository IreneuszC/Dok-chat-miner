Siemens, Rockwell Automation, Mitsubishi Electric, Crestron, HID Global, Gessler GmbH, SystemK, Delta Electronics, Hitron Systems, Qolsys, AVEVA, Chirp Systems, MOVITOOLS, Nice Linear, Integration Objects, Lantronix, and various software have reported vulnerabilities to CISA. These vulnerabilities could allow attackers to gain unauthorized access, execute arbitrary code, escalate privileges, and cause denial-of-service conditions. The companies have provided specific workarounds, mitigations, and updates to reduce the risk of exploitation. CISA recommends implementing cybersecurity strategies, following established procedures, and reporting any suspected malicious activity. No known public exploitation targeting these vulnerabilities has been reported, but organizations are encouraged to take defensive measures.
